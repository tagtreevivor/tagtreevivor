This is my 200+ review of the 2007 American film "The Man from Earth." I am Depresso, a representative of the Soklov tribe in Tagtreevivor. "The Man from Earth" follows John Oldman/Young, a university lecturer, as he leaves the institution. The night before his departure, he invites the other university professors—a biologist, an anthropologist, a specialist in religions, a professor of art history, and a historian—to his home for a farewell party. There, they explore the idea of simply: What would a man's life be like if he were born in the prehistoric era and were still alive today? Instead of presenting its science fiction fantasy genre through presentation, "The Man from Earth" does it through thought. It is unavoidably, purposefully low-key; it is quiet, controlled, and easygoing. The story is told entirely through dialogue between friends and coworkers as they philosophize about their lives and the world at large. There are no action sequences, active flashbacks, or even scenes that take place somewhere else than the house where the characters are assembled. This film explores the "what if" of science fiction in great detail, examining historical details and the viewpoints of various historians and scientists. For history buffs or those who are curious about the possibilities in our world, I heartily suggest this film. Thankyou
